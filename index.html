Greetings Brothers and Sisters.

This document aims to outline and describe the establishment, objectives, and running of a new movement dedicated to the liberation of natives in South Africa. Following the 2024 national elections, it has become clear that political efforts alone cannot end settler minority rule, nor address the longstanding injustices since their arrival on our shores. The current trajectory suggests we are regressing decades, if not centuries, in our struggle for native land ownership and freedom. It is imperative that we explore alternative avenues beyond traditional politics.

The settlers wield numerous powers and advantages that they utilize to advance their conquest of our land. Foremost among these are their financial supremacy, manipulation of politicians as puppets, control of the media, and domination over crucial systems and industries.

The only advantage the natives currently possess is their sheer numbers, yet this advantage remains untapped and ineffective. Much of the native population is unaware of the true state of affairs in our land, primarily due to pervasive issues such as poverty and media propaganda. Various forms of chaos further obstruct the natives from awakening to their reality and liberating themselves from these shackles.

What's particularly disheartening is that even among those natives who are aware of the true state of affairs, there remains a lack of unity and collaboration. Despite recognizing that settlers are sowing division and confusion, many individuals rally behind politicians who clearly prioritize their own interests, those of their families, friends, and financial backers, who happen to be settlers. Conversely, others oppose these politicians, leading to internal strife and chaos within the native community. Meanwhile, the settlers remain organized, united, and steadily advancing their agenda.

I could fill a hundred pages detailing the extent and nature of our current challenges, but that's not the intent of this document. Here, our focus is on proposing solutions that can lead to favorable outcomes and better standard of living for the natives in South Africa: the empowerment of natives to exercise control over both power and land, ensuring equal benefits for all native people.

It's evident that we must harness our numerical advantage to reclaim land and power, and sustainably protect and govern both. This requires uniting the majority of the native population towards these goals. Currently, internal chaos distracts and divides us, preventing collective action. To overcome these challenges, we must eliminate barriers preventing natives from recognizing the true state of affairs. This awakening will ignite concerted efforts towards reclaiming native freedom and land.

This process starts with awakened brothers and sisters who are informed and committed to uniting, working diligently to effect the needed transformation. On social media, I've seen countless reposts and comments where hundreds, if not thousands, of natives challenge and resist settler attempts to undermine us through lies and propaganda. However, our biggest hurdle is our lack of organization. Despite our individual and small-group efforts, many of us, including myself, eventually feel lost and unmotivated, questioning the purpose of our struggle.

I am calling upon you, my brothers and sisters, to unite and organize ourselves to fight for what is rightfully ours.

As previously discussed, we must leverage our numerical advantage to confront the settlers. Currently, there are not enough native individuals who are coming together to collectively reclaim and protect our land, and to gather the necessary strength to sustain it.

Once those who are aware and committed have united and begun working together, our next step will be to awaken the rest of our other brothers and sisters. The campaign will be divided into several sub-campaigns, the details of which will be addressed later. The efforts we will make will target the various reasons and challenges preventing the rest of the native population from becoming aware and joining the struggle, and ensuring that at least the majority of the native population is working together.

Poverty is one of the primary tools used by the settlers to keep the native population in a perpetual state of ignorance about our circumstances. Poverty affects nearly the entire native community. It is for these reasons that I believe one of the tools we must combat first is poverty. We will undertake initiatives that improve the lives of as many natives as possible. Simultaneously, we will educate them about our current situation and inspire them to join the movement. We aim to foster a culture where one native supports another, collectively striving for mutual elevation. Even small advancements or improvements in the lives of our people will significantly contribute to our cause.

I understand that bringing about meaningful change in the lives of natives, especially in combating poverty, requires significant financial resources. Unfortunately, I do not possess such resources alone. However, together, we have the potential to achieve this. My proposal is to establish a primary source of financial strength through monthly contributions from those of us who are able to contribute.

In the past, I have discussed with other natives the idea of pooling contributions to start businesses and generate employment within our communities. I have also observed similar discussions on social media. Concerns often arise about the management of collected funds and ensuring they are used for the collective good. These are legitimate concerns and reflect a healthy skepticism, which I respect. It is disheartening that while our adversaries efficiently organize their resources for their wicked goals, we always encounter more skepticism than support when it comes to funding or working together for our own liberation.

Despite these concerns, I've reached a point where I prioritize the potential benefits over doubts. While I recognize we may not all feel the same, I urge you to temporarily set aside these reservations and envision the great possibilities if our movement succeeds.

I believe we can achieve all that has been mentioned and more, particularly if the majority of us are involved in the running and overseeing of the movement, and we establish stringent measures to prevent mismanagement of the collected financial power. The management of these funds will be transparent within the movement and, to some extent, to the public. I will explain the reasons for this transparency later on.

The collected financial resources will primarily be allocated to initiating and supporting businesses owned by natives. These businesses will serve a crucial role in lifting our people out of poverty and contributing to the growth of our movement through increased manpower and financial resources.

These funds will be used to expand the movement while simultaneously aiding our community in overcoming severe poverty. This support will continue until we achieve the goal of reclaiming our land and ensuring the well-being of every native individual.

The businesses we establish will prioritize essential industries such as food production, materials for shelter, construction, clothing manufacturing, and medicine, among others. These ventures will not only help provide for our basic needs but also serve as educational tools, emphasizing the importance of self-sustenance and utilizing our land effectively.

I acknowledge that the financial contributions from those currently aware of our situation may not suffice to create a substantial impact. We need broader support. There are fellow natives who, while not fully understanding our cause, may recognize the importance of creating employment in impoverished communities or assisting the needy, and could potentially contribute. Unfortunately, due to settler propaganda and misinformation, many may hesitate to support our movement upon learning about it.

To address this challenge, I propose establishing a publicly recognized Non-Profit Organization (NPO/NGO) as the public face of our movement. This organization will serve as a trustworthy entity to collect and manage funds dedicated to our cause. The businesses we initiate or fund will also be publicly profiled through this organization, showcasing the tangible impact of our efforts. This transparency will encourage more support and contributions from the wider community.

Certainly, there's much more to discuss and plan in order to effectively reduce poverty levels while expanding our movement. These efforts will be organized into campaigns known as the "Movement Expansion and Growth Campaign" and the "Movement Financial Management Campaign." Further discussions on these topics can involve input from all our brothers and sisters.

I also want to highlight another significant tool used by the settler to advance their agenda: the media. In South Africa, most, if not all, media outlets are owned and controlled by settlers, who use them to spread lies, propaganda, and misinformation. This has a profound impact on the population, including myself, as I was once a victim of this manipulation. The media stands as one of the most potent weapons in this struggle.

Fortunately, we have a powerful tool on our side: social media. It provides us with the opportunity to counter the settler-controlled media. There are native individuals producing content that exposes lies, educates others about the truth, and challenges propaganda. Some natives may not create content but contribute by commenting, reposting, and liking such content. This demonstrates our potential strength. However, our efforts are often fragmented and lack strategic coordination, unlike the settlers' coordinated media dominance.

I propose launching the "Media Campaign" as our next strategic initiative. The primary goal of this campaign will be to control the narrative and expose truth. Unlike the settlers, our narrative will be based on factual accuracy. We will create and distribute content—written, recorded, and visual media—aimed at native audiences across all social media platforms. Furthermore, this content will be hosted on multiple websites indexed by search engines for easy discovery.

It's essential to counter fabricated narratives like the myth of "white genocide" or false claims about white poverty in South Africa. These falsehoods are deliberately manufactured by settlers to manipulate public opinion, and sadly, some natives fall victim to these distortions. We must act decisively to refute these falsehoods and present the truth without delay.

We possess the numbers, knowledge of the truth, and a wealth of information to share among ourselves as natives. What we urgently need now is unity and strategic coordination to effectively challenge the settler-controlled media advantage.

I cannot wait to work with you.
